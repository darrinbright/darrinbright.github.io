Bio: My research interests focuses on advancing Deep Learning to build intelligent systems capable of understanding and adapting to multimodal environments. I specialize in leveraging neural networks, including convolutional architectures, transformers, and attention mechanisms, to address challenges in computer vision, natural language processing, and multimodal integration. My work aims to develop scalable solutions for automation, virtual environments, and human-centered applications.

Currently, I am working on food portion estimation using 3D reconstruction and feature extraction techniques. Starting with a 2D image, we generate a 3D point cloud representation, and extract features from it. These features are then combined with image features using a cross-attention mechanism and processed through an MLP to predict the nutrients and food volume.

Previously, I have explored diverse topics, including deep learning architectures, object detection and tracking, automated content creation, retrieval-augmented generation systems, and multimodal systems integrating Large Language Models. Beyond academia, I am a cricket enthusiast who enjoys listening to music and playing chess.
